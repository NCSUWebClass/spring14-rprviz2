The Raleigh Public Record is locally focused online newspaper that publishes in depth coverage of local news, especially politics. Not only does it cover issues that traditional media largely ignores, it is at the vanguard of the emerging fusion of journalism and data. For example, they regularly extract publicly available data from its internet backwater and publish visualizations of it, to ground public discourse in facts. They would be excited to have our help in this effort. In particular, they have already published a geographic visualization of free lunch datain the Wake County school system. They would like to see what additional meaning different visualizations of this data might bring to public debate. This project was created by a group of Computer Science students at NC State.

Our visualizations are using D3.js to give better visual representations to Raleigh Public Record.
